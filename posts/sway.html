Switching to Sway


You heard me. I have officially switched from Hyprland to SwayWM.

Why? Because Sway just works. No unnecessary eye candy. No distractions. No constant breaking updates. Just a simple, fast, and efficient tiling window manager.

My Sway Journey
Installing it was easy—just install sway. Then remove Hyprland (which also removes xwayland, so reinstall that if needed).

Configuring Sway is simple too. The syntax is basically i3, so if you’ve ever used i3, you already know what to do. I threw in Waybar, Rofi, and my Everforest dark theme for good measure.

The Philosophy of Sway
Sway doesn’t waste your time with eye candy. No pointless animations, no rounded corners killing battery life, and no blur for the sake of looking "modern." A tiling WM should be minimal and fast—Sway embodies that perfectly.

Why I Switched

  • Sway is actually minimal.
  • I wanted a real wlroots WM.
  • It’s easier to configure.
  • Hyprland kept tempting me to use eye candy. Sway removes that temptation.
  • I wanted to try something new.
  • Hyprland drama, if you care.

The Only Issue: Screen Sharing
OBS and screen sharing needed an extra step. Unlike Hyprland, just installing xdg-desktop-portal and xdg-desktop-portal-wlr doesn’t work. You have to add these to your Sway config: 
exec --no-startup-id /usr/lib/xdg-desktop-portal
exec --no-startup-id /usr/lib/xdg-desktop-portal-wlr

That’s it. No other issues. Everything else just works.

Final Thoughts
Sway is simply better for my needs. If you want a no-nonsense Wayland WM, give it a try.
